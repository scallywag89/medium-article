It was all about the busy backgrounds

If you didn't have a Where's Wally-esque background on your webpage back in the day, what were you even doing?!

UX

In the 90s, people who built websites weren't concerned about the user experience. If the site looked cool, it was enough. People were not walking around with smartphones so responsiveness was not an issue.

It's web-surfin time!

Tiny fonts

For some unknown reason, tiny fonts were popular in the 90s and the change font size was disabled. Today, 16px is common, but back then 10px was standard. It was next to impossible for people over age 40 to read web pages. Web designers figured the only Internet users were young people with good eyesight.
